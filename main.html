First of all, take into consideration the category of your current task. There are three categories:

Category A: You are expected to ask simple questions on a single document. Do not provide more than one pdf file for this task. In order to answer your request properly, consider that the AI models should be able to answer only by analyzing the provided document. Do not expect them to infer on the data.

Category B: Ask hard questions on a single document. This means that the AI models are expected to infer on the provided data. They might need to understand multiple parts of the document in order to give an appropriate answer, or make calculations, for example. The answer shouldn’t be directly obtainable without any “transformation”.

Category C: This is similar to Category B, with the main difference being that multiple documents should be considered in the task. Hard questions are expected here.

Following the guide in the “Getting the documents” section, find 10-Q and/or 10-K reports of the desired companies. Write an original and useful prompt (simple or hard depending on your task’s category) related to the chosen documents.

Provide an ideal answer to your prompt. This is what is expected from a “perfect” AI model. Be careful with the accuracy of the answer and take the time to write properly. You should always be cautious, but especially for the ideal answer, do not make grammatical mistakes!

In order to give such a good answer, what exactly is the information necessary? We need you to write the supporting facts as well. This will help with evaluating the models’ answers later on. Please indicate the document used, the page where the information was found and the fact with any important related information.

Add at least one document to your submission (multiple if your task is of category C) and enter all of the requested information.

Make sure your submission doesn’t have any issues. Take the time to make it perfect! Then submit it.

Please be specific with your prompts! We expect accurate and well formulated prompts for this project. Do not make subjective requests either, the models should follow straightforward instructions. Do not assume the AI models can look up information elsewhere before analyzing the data. Prompts should ask for factual information with support in the documents. Do not ask for opinions, unless they are stated in the documents. That means not asking for an opinion from the AI model itself.

This is a BAD example of a simple prompt:
Do you think this company made enough money last year?

Do not ask the AI models to “think”; making “enough” money is subjective; “last year” can be improved by specifying which year we are talking about”.

You should never include PII (Personally Identifiable Information) in your prompts and answers. PII includes, but is not limited to, any information that can be used to identify an individual, such as names, addresses, phone numbers, social security numbers, email addresses. Sharing or requesting such information will make your submission invalid. Center you prompts about the company itself, not its members.

In 2024, the numbers are respectively $350,018,000,000 and $391,035,000,000. Apple Inc. made 11.72% more than Alphabet Inc. in 2024. For both years, it’s Apple Inc. that generated the most revenues, but Alphabet Inc. grew more.

Considering a yearly increase of (391,035 - 383,285) / 383,285 = 2.02% for Apple Inc. and of (350,018 - 307,394) / 307,394 = 13.87% for Alphabet Inc., here is a breakdown of the potential net sales in the next years:
Company, 2025, 2026

Apple Inc., $398,933,907,000, $406,992,371,921

After only one year, based on the assumption of a stable year-to-year growth for each company, Alphabet Inc. would almost reach the goal. It’s in 2026 that Alphabet Inc. would surpass Apple Inc. though.

Here is the quickest way to obtain the files (see the related screenshots if necessary):

Once on the SEC website (SEC website), find the company you’d like to work on in the “Search By” box. Wait for the page to be fully loaded, it can take a few seconds, otherwise, you might not see the suggestions related to your search. Be patient! If the page is still loading, don’t expect anything to appear. Select the company you were looking for in the search dropdown once it appears.

You will then be sent to the company’s page, on which you can obtain any 10-K/10-Q report available. Click on “View all 10-K’s and 10-Qs” if you are not satisfied with the suggested results. Click on the document you’d like to analyze.

Alternatively, when searching for a company, click on “Submit”. Select the desired company in the list by clicking on their CIK. Specify the filing type, such as “10-K”, and hit “Search”. Click on “Documents” beside what you’re looking for. Select the proper document, in this case (see screenshots), under “Document”, you should click on “aapl-20240928.htm”. This is not as straightforward as the previous method, but it can be done this way, especially if no dropdown menu appears when searching for a company (keep in mind that it might take some time before being able to see the dropdown menu, the page needs to be fully loaded!).

Finally, to obtain the pdf file of this report, click on “Menu” in the upper left corner of the page, then “Open as HTML”. Save the link, this is what you need to enter for “Link to the SEC page”. Print the page, save it as PDF, and you’re good to go.
